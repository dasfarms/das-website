Cold Tolerent Gardenias

Most Gardenia can be grown in zone 8 and warmer. Their are, however, 2 or 3 cultivars that can flourish in considerable colder climates. Two of these are Daisy and Frost Proof. We hope that gardeners in zone 6 and 7 will try these wonderful selections that are both beautiful and fragrant. To make this as painless as possible we are placing both on sale

Daisy Gardenia

This is a hardy gardenia and can be grown much further north than most gardenias. Daisy Gardenia produces masses of fragrant, single, daisy-shaped, white flowers in late Spring/early Summer and sporadically through the season. Blooms heavier than other gardenias.

Frost Proof Gardenia

A more upright habit, supporting shiny, dark green leaves and fragrant, white 2- to 3-inch wide flowers make this a fine hedge or foundation plant that thrives with more direct sun than other varieties. Flower buds resist late spring frosts without damage and it's evergreen. 'Frost Proof' is a prolific bloomer of fragrant 2-3" flowers.

Plant Early

Early spring is a good time to plant as long as the ground is not frozen. In early spring, roots begin new growth and continue to develop at a fast rate, and a little later top growth begins. While the same plant planted in late spring gets a slow start due root adjustment, the early-planted plants are becoming well established. When summer finally arrives, the early-planted plant is far better equipped to deal with heat and drought, largely due to its well-established root system.
